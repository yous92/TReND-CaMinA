# Introduction to computational neuroscience - reading list 
By Marcus Ghosh and Dan Goodman. 

## Introduction
* [For Hodgkin & Huxley (1952) see Raman & Ferster (2022) "The annotated Hodgkin and Huxley: A reader's guide"](https://press.princeton.edu/books/paperback/9780691220635/the-annotated-hodgkin-and-Huxley)
* [Lengyel (2024) "Marr's three levels of analysis are useful as a framework for neuroscience"](https://doi.org/10.1113/JP279549)
* [Yamins et al., (2014) "Performance-optimized hierarchical models predict neural responses in higher visual cortex"](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1403112111)
* [Huys et al., (2016) "Computational psychiatry as a bridge from neuroscience to clinical applications"](https://doi.org/10.1038/nn.4238)
* [Hassabis et al., (2017) "Neuroscience-Inspired Artificial Intelligence"](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2017.06.011)
* [Sorrell et al., (2021) "Brain–machine interfaces: Closed-loop control in an adaptive system"](https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-control-061720-012348)
* [Jones & Kording (2017) "Could a neuroscientist understand a microprocessor?"](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pcbi.1005268)


## Simulating single neurons
* [Mensi et al. (2012) "Parameter extraction and classification of three cortical neuron types reveals two distinct adaptation mechanisms"](https://doi.org/10.1152/jn.00408.2011)
* [Goodman & Brette (2008) "Brian: a simulator for spiking neural networks in Python"](https://doi.org/10.3389/neuro.11.005.2008)

## Simulating networks of neurons 
* [McCulloch & Pitts (1943) "A logical calculus of the ideas immanent in nervous activity"](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF02478259)
* [Piccinini (2004) "The first computational theory of mind and brain: A close look at McCulloch and Pitts' Logical calculus of ideas immanent in nervous activity"](https://philpapers.org/rec/PICTFC)
* [Rosenblatt (1958) "The perceptron: A probabilistic model for information storage and organization in the brain."](https://psycnet.apa.org/doi/10.1037/h0042519)
* [Bishop & Bishop (2024) "Deep learning"](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-031-45468-4)
* [Zenke et al., (2021) "Visualizing a joint future of neuroscience and neuromorphic engineering"](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2021.01.009)
